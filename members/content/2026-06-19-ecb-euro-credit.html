The ECB's June decision was less about the move itself and more about the signal: the bar to further easing is now higher, and the Council is comfortable letting the data lead. For euro credit, that has three implications.

Spreads

Investment-grade euro spreads have little room to tighten from here on fundamentals alone, but a benign rate path caps the downside. We would treat the asset class as carry rather than capital gain.

Duration

With the front end anchored, the better risk-reward sits in the belly of the curve. We are extending duration selectively in high-grade names with resilient cash flows.

Where we would add

Financials senior paper and defensive non-cyclicals screen well. We remain cautious on lower-rated cyclicals where refinancing walls and margin pressure coincide.
