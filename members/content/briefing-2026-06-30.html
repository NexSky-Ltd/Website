› UK political transition: PM Starmer resigned 22 June; Andy Burnham entered the Labour leadership race, raising fiscal-loosening risk. GBP/USD near 2026 lows at 1.3181; gilts face upward yield pressure if Burnham relaxes spending rules.
› UK macro deterioration: CBI retail sales balance fell to -54 in June from -46 in May; manufacturing order book at -45, lowest since 2020. Stagflation signal reinforces gilt vulnerability and limits BoE optionality.
› USD/JPY at 162.20, testing the upper bound of the range at which BoJ verbal intervention has historically intensified. A sharp yen move would pressure carry trades and EM FX broadly.

Macro
UST 10Y holds at 4.38% with the 2s10s curve at +28bp, steeper than Q1 but not yet signalling aggressive re-pricing of cuts. The Fed's first meeting under Chair Kevin Warsh concluded mid-June; no rate change was delivered and no speakers are scheduled today. PCE rose 0.4% in May (core +0.3%), keeping the Fed on hold; Q1 GDP was revised up to 2.1% annualised. The flash US manufacturing PMI hit 55.7 in June, a four-year high, but employment softened for a second month and input prices remain historically elevated on tariff and energy pass-through. Chicago PMI (June) and CB Consumer Confidence print today; both feed the growth-vs-inflation framing the Fed is navigating.
