The risk the June view flagged as rising started to play out. In the week of 19 to 26 June a sharp, narrow sell-off hit the AI complex: the Nasdaq 100 fell 4.2%, semiconductors 7.7% and the Magnificent Seven 5.9%, while the equal-weight S&P rose 1.6%, the Russell 2000 1.0% and healthcare 7.9%. That is the broadening and the defensive rotation we have been positioned for, triggered by doubts over whether more than 450 billion dollars of 2026 hyperscaler capex earns a proportionate return. The Fed, under Warsh, held at 3.50 to 3.75% on 17 June but turned its dot plot to a hike for 2026 and lifted its year-end PCE projection to 3.6%. Hawkish-for-longer is now in the projections, not just the rhetoric.

Risk then stabilised into month-end: the Nasdaq rebounded 2.1% on 30 June and the S&P closed the month near 7,440, with the 10-year Treasury steady at 4.38%, Brent at 73.5 dollars, EUR/USD at 1.14 and USD/JPY at 162. The bounce is consistent with our read that late June was a valuation reset, not a fundamental break, and does not change the direction of travel.

We are not changing the architecture of the view, we are leaning further into it. We stay constructive on equities but more selective, tilted to quality, defensives and non-US. We keep short duration with a belly and quality hedge for the growth-scare case. We hold gold through a deep pullback and would top up. We strengthen the long-dollar and short-high-yield calls, and we turn modestly more constructive on energy as Middle East risk re-prices. The two adverse regimes now outweigh the continuation path for the first time.

1 · Macro and geopolitical outlook

No anointed base case. The next 12 months remain multi-modal, and this month the balance shifted toward the two adverse regimes, which now total 55%. The map is mutually exclusive and sums to 100.

2 · Key trends, next 12 months

  1. Hawkish-for-longer is now in the dots, not just the talk. The June projection flipped the 2026 median to a hike and raised year-end PCE to 3.6%. Cash and short-duration carry rewarded, long duration vulnerable, except in the air-pocket where it is a hedge.
  2. AI capex supercycle meets its first real valuation test. Combined 2026 hyperscaler capex above 450 billion dollars with no proportionate return yet identified; the late-June reset was a valuation reset, not a fundamental break. Selectivity over beta.
  3. Equity broadening, confirmed. The sharpest growth-versus-value divergence of the year: value roughly flat, growth down 3.7% in a week, defensives and small caps up. Diversify beyond US mega-cap.
  4. Energy and geopolitics back as the swing factor. The ceasefire is fragile and energy re-entered the Fed's inflation reasoning. Oil is again the dominant two-way driver of inflation and risk appetite.
  5. Dollar firm on a widening rate gap. The Fed turning to a hike against an ECB whose surveyed inflation expectations are easing to 3.5% widens the differential in the dollar's favour.

3 · Structural and long-term trends

  1. AI, power and compute. A multi-year capex and electricity-demand supercycle; own the value chain, not just mega-cap.
  2. Fiscal dominance and high public debt. Structurally higher term premia, periodic bond-market stress, and a tailwind for gold.
  3. Multipolarity and de-globalisation. Reshoring, defence, commodities and a persistent geopolitical risk premium.
  4. Central-bank gold accumulation. Central banks bought 244 tonnes in Q1 2026, above the five-year average; a structural bid under gold through its drawdown.
  5. Institutionalisation of family-office capital. Allocations converging with institutions, especially in private markets at about 30%.

4 · Tactical view, one to three months

Constructive but more selective. Risk-on equities tilted to quality, defensives and non-US; short duration with a belly hedge; gold and a long dollar as hedges; modestly more constructive on energy.

Stay long equities but keep broadening into Japan, EM equity, Europe, quality cyclicals, financials, industrials and the AI value chain, and keep trimming US mega-cap concentration. Run short and belly duration for carry, and add belly and quality bonds as the air-pocket hedge. Hold the strategic 5% gold through this dip and use the pullback to top up toward target. Stay long the dollar through the FX overlay. Lift the energy-equity tail hedge modestly on renewed Hormuz risk.

Duration caveat: our underweight-duration tilt is a muddle-through and stagflation call. In the AI air-pocket, a growth scare with benign inflation, long duration rallies, so that regime is hedged by the belly, quality bonds and the 5% gold, not by the short-duration tilt. With the air-pocket probability now at 30%, that hedge matters more this month.

5 · Key risks to the thesis

  1. AI capex disappoints on ROI. Spend ahead of monetisation; a retrenchment takes semis, power demand and the earnings base with it. Our single biggest structural risk, now part-realised.
  2. Iran and Hormuz re-escalation. A fragile ceasefire; a breakdown reopens the oil and inflation tail and forces harder tightening into a slowdown.
  3. Fiscal and bond-market accident. A buyers' strike in Treasuries or gilts spikes term premia and resets every discount rate.
  4. Fed credibility under Warsh. A policy error or perceived loss of independence un-anchors inflation expectations and the dollar.
  5. Dollar and reserve shock. A disorderly dollar move or faster reserve diversification that breaks cross-asset correlations.
