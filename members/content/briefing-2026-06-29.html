Geopolitical
› US futures climbed at the open on Iran peace-talk headlines, partially reversing last week's risk-off pressure; Brent closed Friday at USD 73.06/bbl, +1.49%, recovering from the prior-session low of USD 73.74/bbl — the lowest since before February airstrikes.
› A bipartisan Senate majority voted 50–48 on a war powers resolution to end US involvement in Iran hostilities; four Republicans crossed the aisle — market implication is further downward pressure on energy risk premia if momentum toward de-escalation holds.
› US government cleared Anthropic to release its Claude 'Mythos 5' model on June 26, and the Trump administration was reportedly close to restoring access to the 'Fable 5' model as of June 27; near-term implication is incremental competitive pressure on OpenAI and cloud-based AI incumbents.
› EU MiCA full compliance deadline falls Wednesday July 1, marking end of transitional period for crypto exchanges and stablecoin issuers in Europe; non-compliant platforms face service restrictions — BTC at USD 59,555 reflects ongoing macro and regulatory headwinds.
Macro
PCE for May printed 4.1% YoY — the highest since April 2023 — with core at 3.4% YoY, the highest since October 2023; both in line with consensus but firmly above the Fed's 2% target. The FOMC held at 3.50%–3.75% for a fourth consecutive meeting on June 17, removed its prior rate-cut signal, and flagged a hike as likely. UST 10Y closed Friday at 4.38%, -2bp on the session, with the 2s10s curve at +31bp; the curve steepened modestly on the 30Y printing +1bp to 4.87%, consistent with hike-then-hold pricing. University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment confirmed at 48.9 for June final — near historic lows — compressing the growth-versus-inflation trade-off the Fed faces. NFP on Thursday July 2 is the week's dominant data event; initial jobless claims of 215,000 for the week ended June 20 suggest labour remains tight, which would reinforce the hawkish Warsh stance.
Corporate
› Accenture (ACN) — shares closed down nearly 18%, the steepest single-day decline in the company's history, following earnings; market cap destruction ranked among the largest single-session losses in S&P 500 services history.
› Nvidia (NVDA) — stock fell an additional 1% Friday, capping an 8% weekly decline, its worst week in over a year; the move reflects a broader mega-cap AI valuation reset following cost-concern headlines.
› ON Semiconductor (ON) — plunged 14% after announcing an all-stock acquisition of Synaptics (SYNA), a human-interface chip specialist, for USD 7B; Synaptics rose 3.7% on the deal premium.
› OpenAI — executives reportedly considering delaying IPO to 2027 given choppy global markets and post-IPO weakness in SpaceX (SPCX) shares; Oracle (ORCL) and CoreWeave (CRWV) fell on the news as both had been positioned as OpenAI ecosystem beneficiaries.
› Apple (AAPL) and Microsoft (MSFT) — both announced price increases on iPhone and Xbox product lines, triggering Magnificent 7 selling on June 25; rotation out of mega-cap tech into other sectors accelerated into week-end.
