Geopolitical
› Strait of Hormuz traffic remains at ~5% of pre-conflict levels; with 20M bbl/day historically transiting the strait (~20% of global petroleum liquids), every week of disruption compounds supply-side pressure on Brent and industrial input costs.
› USMCA six-year review deadline falls today, 1 July; if no extension is agreed, the agreement auto-continues to 2036 with an annual review mechanism — watch for US/Canada/Mexico official statements before NY open for any tariff or trade-flow repricing.
› Iran ceasefire holds in week 12-ish post-April 7 announcement, but no resumption of normalised shipping; ISM May data showed Iran conflict cited in 42% of manufacturing survey responses, keeping goods-price disinflation at risk.
› Russia-Ukraine conflict continues with no named 24h development; broader energy market volatility remains the principal channel into European rates and EUR/USD.
Macro
UST 10Y at 4.44%, +6bp on the session, with the 2s10s curve at +30bp and the 30Y at 4.91% — the long end is leading the selloff. The Fed held rates at its June meeting; markets price ~79% probability of at least one cut in 2026, but hot May PCE and a 54% ISM Manufacturing print in May leave that path conditional on sequential softening. Fed Chair Warsh speaks at the ECB Sintra symposium today — his second appearance this week — and any deviation from the June hold posture will reprice the front end sharply. June NFP releases Thursday (a day early ahead of the 4 July closure); consensus sits at 172,000, and a print above 200K would extend the rates selloff into the holiday. Bund 10Y held flat at 2.92%, widening the UST-Bund spread to +152bp, reflecting diverging growth and policy trajectories.
Corporate
› Devon Energy (DVN) / Coterra Energy (CTRA) — merger creates one of the largest US shale producers combining Permian, Eagle Ford, and Marcellus acreage; deal continues the post-2024 onshore consolidation wave; both stocks moved in line with energy sector on close.
› Alphabet (GOOGL) / Intersect Power — acquisition of the clean-power developer adds multiple GW of commissioned solar and storage capacity; signals hyperscalers vertically integrating into power generation rather than buying from utilities; GOOGL closed in line with Nasdaq +1.52%.
› Sun Pharma / Organon — largest biotech deal of 2026 to date; India's Sun Pharma acquired Merck spin-off Organon to build a global women's-health and biosimilars platform; establishes Sun as a top-10 global generics player.
› HPE (HPE) / Juniper antitrust controversy — DOJ settlement faces allegations of political interference after former AAG reportedly rejected the deal before the AG's office overruled Antitrust Division opposition; two deputies subsequently dismissed; regulatory risk premium re-rating in progress for pending M&A.
