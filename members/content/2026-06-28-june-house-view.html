Our base case is unchanged: growth slows without breaking, inflation grinds lower in fits and starts, and policy rates have peaked across the major blocs. That backdrop argues for adding duration of quality while keeping risk assets at benchmark rather than reaching.

Strategic stance

We hold the strategic allocation steady this month. The case for diversification away from concentrated equity beta strengthens as valuations price a soft landing that is far from assured.

  • Equities: neutral, with a tilt toward quality and cash-generative balance sheets.
  • Rates: overweight high-grade duration; we prefer 5 to 10 year sovereigns.
  • Credit: up in quality; investment grade over high yield at current spreads.
  • Alternatives: maintain private credit and selective real assets for income and diversification.

Tactical tilts

We add modestly to high-grade duration and trim a portion of the cyclical equity overweight accumulated through the spring. Cash continues to earn its keep as dry powder for dislocations.

What would change our mind

A re-acceleration in core inflation, a disorderly move in long-end yields, or a credit event in the leveraged-loan complex would each prompt a reassessment. We are watching the labour data and bank lending standards most closely.
