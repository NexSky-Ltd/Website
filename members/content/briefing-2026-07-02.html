Geopolitical
› Iran — Ghalibaf walked out of Doha talks on 1 July, stating Tehran will not resume direct negotiations with Washington until the $6B Qatari-held funds dispute is resolved; Hormuz shipping traffic remains at 5% of pre-conflict levels, sustaining a structural risk premium in energy and tanker names.
› Iran sanctions — Tehran's temporary US oil-transaction licence expires 21 August; any escalation before that date tightens the energy sanctions framework and is a fresh upside catalyst for Brent.
› Iraq — Security forces detained 47 individuals including a deputy oil minister in a Green Zone anti-corruption sweep on 28 June; adds to sub-regional instability signal without direct Hormuz impact.
› Lebanon — Hezbollah's rejection of the Israel peace framework introduces another layer of Levant instability; risk is containment failure lifting regional risk premium across credit and energy.
Macro
UST 10Y sits at 4.48%, +4bp session-on-session, with the 30Y at 4.97% and 2s10s at +31bp — the long end is leading the sell-off. Fed Chair Warsh said at his inaugural June 17 FOMC press conference that labour data "has been moving in a good direction"; BofA now calls for three hikes in 2026 contingent on continued strength. Today's June NFP (consensus +115k) is the last major US print before Friday's market closure for Fourth of July. ADP for June showed uneven private hiring, with leisure and hospitality posting a sixth consecutive weak month — a downside flag relative to May's +172k surprise. US HY OAS at 275bp and IG at 76bp show credit is not yet pricing a deterioration in the labour backdrop.
Corporate
› Nike (NKE) — CEO Elliott Hill stated there has been 'nothing normal' about the retail environment as the brand's multi-year reset continues; shares fell 3.51% in premarket Wednesday despite a headline earnings beat, closing the session lower.
› Netflix (NFLX) / Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) — $83B merger, the largest deal on the 2026 board, remains on track for H2 close pending shareholder and regulatory approval; would create the only true scale streaming-plus-Hollywood-library platform.
› Electronic Arts (EA) / PIF-Silver Lake-Affinity consortium — $55B acquisition, the largest video-game deal in history, remains in CFIUS review; outcome will set precedent for SWF-led acquisitions of US IP-heavy assets.
› Charter Communications (CHTR) / Cox Communications — $34.5B merger continues to await regulatory clearance, adding to a concentrated queue of large-cap media and telecom consolidations pending in H2 2026.
